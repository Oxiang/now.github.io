The Pomodoro Technique



Increase your productivity by dividing your workday into highly focused chunks separated by short breaks (called "Pomodoros").


This trains your brain to focus for short periods and helps you stay on top of deadlines or your consistently full e-mail inbox. With time it can even help improve your attention span and concentration.

The Pomodoro Technique



Increase your productivity by dividing your workday into highly focused chunks separated by short breaks (called "Pomodoros").


This trains your brain to focus for short periods and helps you stay on top of deadlines or your consistently full e-mail inbox. With time it can even help improve your attention span and concentration.


You work in short sprints

You take regular breaks


Work 25 Minutes

Say this to yourself, "I will not allow myself to get distracted. I will spend the next 25 minutes on this task without any interruptions." You can do it! After all, it's just 25 minutes.


Rest 5 Minutes

Well done! You’ve spent an entire, interruption-less Pomodoro on a task. Breathe, meditate, grab a cup of coffee, go for a short walk or do anything relaxing (i.e., not work-related). Your brain will thank you later.


Repeat

Once you’ve completed four pomodoros, you can take a longer break. 20 minutes is good. Or 30. Your brain will use this time to digest the new information and rest before the next round of Pomodoros.
