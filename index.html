More than ever, our generation is facing distractions like never before. Social media and entertainment contents have infiltrated our lives in every single aspect. We find it harder to get important task done and we are frustrated at our own incapability.


In the age of distractions, having control over your productivity is the key to success.

The Pomodoro Technique



Increase your productivity by dividing your workday into highly focused chunks separated by short breaks (called "Pomodoros").



This trains your brain to focus for short periods and helps you stay on top of deadlines or constantly-refilling inboxes. With time it can even help improve your attention span and concentration.


You work in short sprints

You take regular breaks

Work 25 Minutes

Make a small oath to yourself: I will spend 25 minutes on this task and I will not interrupt myself. You can do it! After all, it’s just 25 minutes.

Rest 5 Minutes

Well done! You’ve spent an entire, interruption-less Pomodoro on a task. Breathe, meditate, grab a cup of coffee, go for a short walk or do something else relaxing (i.e., not work-related). Your brain will thank you later.

Repeat

Once you’ve completed four pomodoros, you can take a longer break. 20 minutes is good. Or 30. Your brain will use this time to assimilate new information and rest before the next round of Pomodoros.
