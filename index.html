The World of Distraction


More than ever, our generation is facing distractions like never before. Social media and entertainment contents have infiltrated our lives in every single aspect.

We find it harder to get important task done and we are frustrated at our own incapability.

Pomodoro Technique

1. Work 25 Minutes


Make a small oath to yourself: I will spend 25 minutes on this task and I will not interrupt myself. You can do it! After all, it’s just 25 minutes.

2. Rest 5 Minutes


Congratulations! You’ve spent an entire, interruption-less Pomodoro on a task. Breathe, meditate, grab a cup of coffee, go for a short walk or do something else relaxing (i.e., not work-related). Your brain will thank you later.

3. Repeat


Once you’ve completed four pomodoros, you can take a longer break. 20 minutes is good. Or 30. Your brain will use this time to assimilate new information and rest before the next round of Pomodoros.
